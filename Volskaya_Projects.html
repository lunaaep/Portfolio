Bridge Designer Project

The objective of the project was to create a functioning bridge design that would be stable enough to carry a load, while facing constraints such as build cost, material cost, and individual material properties. It was interesting to get to know the bridge building software, as my partner and I were both new to it at first. This definitely brought some challenges in the beginning phases of our project. Another difficult aspect of the project was to figure out where to sacrifice stability for a lower cost. PLTW 1.3.1 Dangerous Situation

November 4 - November 7, 2022 for PLTW Cybersecurity


The objective of this project was to use an PLTW emulator machine to virtually discover cybersecurity breaches and fix them. Our class worked in pairs of two to act as a cybersecurity team and remove the vulnerabilities from the computer. To prepare for the project, we learned about the different types of vulnerabilities, how they may be hidden, and how to find/remove them. In the duration of the project, my partner and I found twelve vulnerabilities on the machine and fixed them, documenting the state of the computer before and after. Each stage was related to the Cybersecurity Lifecycle. After reflecting, I learned that small issues that are easily overlooked can turn into large vulnerabilities that put the system at risk, and so I will be more wary of similar issues on my own devices. A link to the to the documentation and rubric can be found here.


PLTW 3.1.4 Find the Secrets

March 1 - March 5, 2023 for PLTW Cybersecurity


In this project, our cybersecurity class worked in pairs to discover vulnerabilities in a Linux based system. Similarly to the previous project, we launched a virtual lab in an emulator and opened up the shell for two users - Gamma and Delta, which were the only legitimate users on the network. Using the Linux commands we had learned in previous activities, the potential threats were removed for the safety of the network. It was discovered that Gamma and Delta are on one network also containing malicious users Epsilon and Zeta, which were running harmful processes from hidden start-up files and creating their own files in directories as well. We used commands such as cd, ps, kill, su, and rm to eliminate the threats and their potential to do harm, categorizing our steps with the Cybersecurity Lifecycle along the way.
